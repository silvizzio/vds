Modals interrupt. Use them only when the task must be finished or abandoned
before continuing, such as confirming a destructive action or capturing a
required decision. For non-blocking information, prefer a toast or inline panel.

## When to use

- Confirming an irreversible action (delete, reset, publish).
- Capturing a short, required input that blocks the current flow.
- Surfacing a focused sub-task without leaving the current view.

## When not to use

- Passive notifications or status. Use a toast.
- Long forms or multi-step flows. Use a dedicated page or drawer.
- Content the operator needs while working in the scene behind it.

## Guidance

<DoDont>
  <Do>Name the specific object in the title: Delete twin snapshot, not Are you sure?</Do>
  <Dont>Rely on a generic title and bury the object in body text.</Dont>
  <Do>Put the primary action last and match its label to the title's verb.</Do>
  <Dont>Auto-focus the destructive action or let Enter trigger it by default.</Dont>
</DoDont>

## Behavior

- Escape and scrim click both dismiss, and they map to Cancel, never to the destructive action.
- Focus moves into the panel on open and returns to the trigger on close.
- The panel width caps at sm/md/lg. Content scrolls inside rather than growing the panel past the viewport.

<Accessibility>

## Roles and structure

- Panel uses `role="dialog"` with `aria-modal="true"`.
- `aria-labelledby` points at the title, `aria-describedby` at the description when present.

## Keyboard

- **Escape** closes the modal and returns focus to the trigger.
- **Tab** cycles focusable elements. Extend with a full focus trap before shipping to production.

## Focus management

- On open, focus moves to the panel.
- On close, focus returns to the element that opened it.

## Motion

- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Keep enter and exit transitions short and non-essential.

</Accessibility>
